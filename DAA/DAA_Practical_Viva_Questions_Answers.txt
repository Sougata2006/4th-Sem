DAA Practical Viva Questions and Answers

1.  Binary Search 

Q: What is the prerequisite for Binary Search? A: The
    array must be sorted.

Q: Why is Binary Search faster than Linear Search? A: It reduces the
search space by half in every step.

Q: Which technique does it use? A: Divide and Conquer.

Q: Can Binary Search work on a linked list? A: No, because efficient
random access is not available.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.  Merge Sort 

Q: Why is Merge Sort called Divide and Conquer? A: It
    divides the array, recursively sorts both halves, and merges them.

Q: Is Merge Sort stable? A: Yes.

Q: Is Merge Sort in-place? A: No.

Q: Why is Merge Sort preferred for linked lists? A: Merging linked lists
is efficient without shifting elements.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.  Quick Sort 

Q: Which pivot is used in your program? A: The last
    element.

Q: Is Quick Sort stable? A: No.

Q: Is Quick Sort in-place? A: Yes.

Q: Why can Quick Sort become O(N²)? A: Poor pivot selection causes
highly unbalanced partitions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.  Find Min-Max 

Q: Why use Divide and Conquer? A: It reduces
    comparisons.

Q: Can this be solved iteratively? A: Yes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.  BFS 

Q: Which data structure is used? A: Queue.

Q: Why Queue? A: BFS visits vertices level by level (FIFO).

Q: Can BFS find the shortest path? A: Yes, in an unweighted graph.

Q: Does your implementation use recursion? A: No.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

6.  DFS 

Q: Which data structure is used? A: Stack.

Q: Can DFS be recursive? A: Yes.

Q: BFS vs DFS? A: - BFS: Queue, level-wise traversal. - DFS:
Stack/Recursion, depth-wise traversal.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

7.  Dijkstra 

Q: Can it handle negative edge weights? A: No.

Q: Which algorithm handles negative weights? A: Bellman-Ford.

Q: Which technique is used? A: Greedy.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

8.  Bellman-Ford 

Q: Can it handle negative edges? A: Yes.

Q: Can it detect negative cycles? A: Yes.

Q: Why relax edges V-1 times? A: A longest simple path contains at most
V-1 edges.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

9.  Floyd-Warshall

Q: Which problem does it solve? A: All-Pairs Shortest
    Path.

Q: Which technique is used? A: Dynamic Programming.

Q: Can it handle negative edges? A: Yes.

Q: Can it handle negative cycles? A: No.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

10. Prim’s Algorithm 

Q: Which technique is used? A: Greedy.

Q: What does it find? A: Minimum Spanning Tree (MST).

Q: Can it work on directed graphs? A: No.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

11. Kruskal’s Algorithm 

Q: Which data structure is used? A: Union-Find
    (Disjoint Set).

Q: Why sort edges? A: To select the smallest edge first.

Q: How are cycles detected? A: Using Union-Find.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

12. Fractional Knapsack 

Q: Which technique is used? A: Greedy.

Q: Why sort by Profit/Weight ratio? A: It maximizes profit.

Q: Can we take fractions of items? A: Yes.

Q: Does Greedy work for 0/1 Knapsack? A: No.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

13. Graph Coloring 

Q: Which technique is used? A: Backtracking.

Q: Main rule? A: Adjacent vertices cannot have the same color.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

14. Hamiltonian Cycle 

Q: Hamiltonian vs Euler Cycle? A:

-   Hamiltonian: Visits every vertex once.
-   Euler: Visits every edge once.

Q: Technique? A: Backtracking.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

15. N-Queen 

Q: Which technique is used? A: Backtracking.

Q: What conditions are checked? A: Same column and both diagonals.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

16. Travelling Salesman Problem (TSP) 

Q: Which technique is used? A:
    Backtracking.

Q: Why is it difficult? A: It explores many possible tours (factorial
time).

Q: Hamiltonian vs TSP? A: Hamiltonian checks existence of a cycle; TSP
finds the minimum-cost cycle.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
